I believe in creating an engaging learning environment that bridges theory and practice.

Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy centers on the belief that management education should not only impart theoretical knowledge but also develop critical thinking and practical decision-making skills. I strive to create an interactive classroom environment where students are encouraged to question assumptions, analyze real-world business scenarios, and develop evidence-based solutions.

I employ a variety of pedagogical tools including case studies, simulations, group projects, and data analytics exercises to ensure that students can apply theoretical concepts to practical business challenges. My goal is to prepare students to become thoughtful, ethical, and effective leaders in their respective organizations.
